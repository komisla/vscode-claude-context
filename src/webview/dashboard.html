How context fill affects response quality

The 40% and 60% thresholds are quality heuristics, not Anthropic product limits.

  1. Lost in the Middle effect - Liu et al. 2023: recall degrades measurably for information in the middle of long contexts. Beginning and end are retrieved far more reliably. Source
  2. Effective context is much smaller than claimed context - NoLiMa benchmark, Modarressi et al. 2025: 11 of 13 models reach at most half their baseline score at 32K tokens. Claude 3.5 Sonnet measured effective length: about 8K despite a 200K window. Source
  3. Context rot is a gradient, not a cliff - Anthropic Engineering: finite attention spreads thinner as context grows; quality degrades well before 100%. Source
  4. Practical guideline - Anthropic Help Center recommends /clear between tasks and /compact for long-running sessions. Auto-compact default: about 95%. Source
  5. Fill % includes cache reads - used_percentage = input_tokens + cache_creation + cache_read. A session can appear full even when most tokens are cached, not new content.
